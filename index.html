Executive Summary

  • BYD (Build Your Dreams) — world's largest NEV producer, selling over 3 million EVs globally in 2024 (Statista, 2025)
  • Vertically integrated across batteries, semiconductors, motors, and vehicle manufacturing
  • The US–Iran conflict and energy crisis are accelerating the global shift toward EVs, creating major opportunities for BYD
  • This presentation covers PESTLE, Porter's Five Forces, STP strategy, competitor comparison, challenges, and success drivers

Porter's Five Forces

New Entrants Moderate

High capital barriers, but Apple & Xiaomi entering via platform advantages

Supplier Power Low

BYD's vertical integration — in-house battery, chip & motor production

Buyer Power Moderate

Growing choices & price sensitivity in mass-market EV segment

Substitutes Moderate

Hydrogen fuel cells, public transport & hybrid vehicles as alternatives

Rivalry High

Intense competition: Tesla, Toyota, VW, NIO, Hyundai — price wars & tech race (Porter, 1980)

Challenges and Barriers

🏷️ Brand Recognition

Low awareness in Western markets; needs major marketing investment (Keller, 2013)

🌐 Geopolitical Risk

EU tariffs up to 38.1% (European Commission, 2024); US–Iran conflict disrupting energy supply & shipping routes

🔍 Quality Perception

"Made in China" stigma in premium segments; trust-building required

🏪 Distribution

Underdeveloped dealer & service networks in Europe and Americas

🌏 Cultural Adaptation

Must localise marketing & design for diverse regional preferences

📜 Regulation

Complex compliance across markets: data privacy, safety, environment
